Your Proposals

City is made not only from open spaces and buildings – it is a local community first of all. People use public spaces together, adopt them to own needs and add social meaning and value. Henri Lefebvre in his pamphlet Right to the city (1968) asks: "Which are the socially successful places and which will they be? How do we recognize them? Based on what criteria?" . While answering to these questions Alexander Ståhle created sociotop mapping method in 2000 as an urban planning tools which allows to identify use and social values of public spaces objectively. Sociotop method till now is successfully and widely used in urban planning Sweden.

The aim of the "Present map“ of Šanciai is to collect information about the contemporary your activities in public spaces, their use and social values based on the Sociotop method. This map will become an important corner stone for the vision of Šančiai.
